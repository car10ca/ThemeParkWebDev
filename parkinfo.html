Park Information

What To Expect

Virtuality is the world's first electronic gaming theme park. Our modern building was completed in 2020 and fully air-conditioned / heated and wheelchair friendly.

We cover 4 main areas in the gaming world: Retro, eSports, Casino and in particular Virtual Reality.

On this page, you'll find detailed information on tickets and reservations, how to get here as well as answers to some of the most frequently asked questions.

Tickets and Reservations

Tickets can be purchased from the gates on your arrival, however, we recommend that you book online to guarantee a spot and skip the lines.

How to get here

About 5 minutes from Springfield on the I72, you will pass our signature rooftops on the left. You really can't miss it. Once past, take the next turnoff to Castle Rock. Right at the first traffic light and then just follow the billboards to the parking area.

You can also find us on Apple Maps, Google Maps or Bing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why don't you provide a more detailed map of the park?

We're constantly upgrading our consoles, arcade and virtual realtiy machines. There's also often a change in interest in our experiences to which we adjust accordingly. This means moving the items around often to make sure everyone can get the most out of all experiences whilst they visit.

Keep an eye out for our neon on park signage like the one throughout this page. These are always kept up-to-date.

Alternatively you can ask any of our staff members for help.

Where can I smoke?

Since Virtuality is mostly an indoor theme park, you might be worried about where to smoke. Luckily, we are so retro, we don't really care where you smoke as long as you don't start a fire or burn other visitors.

Vintage spinning ashtrays from the 80s are provided throughout, or otherwise you can just ash on the carpets.

Out of cigarettes? No problem, we have vending machines by the toilets that accept cash, apple pay, google pay, android pay, pay pal, visa and mastercard.

Do you have WiFi?

Yes, we do. WiFi is provided throughout the park free of charge.

For security reasons, we require that you create an account to use the internet. Usage is unlimited but we restrict users from downloading large files (above 500MB) such as long videos. This is to ensure that all our visitors can enjoy the internet without any delay.

We understand that many people work remotely these days and are required to remain available at all times. For this purpose, it is possible to purchase a two hour, unlimited data internet pass. You can purchase this directly on our app.

Where can I eat?

Each of our 4 sectors contain a foodcourt area designated on our map and with signposts inside the park.

Our food courts all cover an extensive range of international cuisine, so you're sure to find something suitable to your taste.

Some of the foods offered are: Pizza, Sushi, Noodles, Sweets, Artisan Burgers, Surf and Turf, Seafood, Vegan, Vegetarian, Ice Cream, Coffee and Cakes, Mexican, Indian, Turkish, the list goes on.

To protect our equipment, please be advised that food can't be consumed whilst in the gaming halls. However, we do allow the consumption of drinks given they are kept in a bottle with a fully closable lid to avoid spills.
